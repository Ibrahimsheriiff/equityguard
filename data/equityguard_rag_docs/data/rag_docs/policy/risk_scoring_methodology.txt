Title: EquityGuard Risk Scoring Methodology
Document Type: Methodology
Version: 1.0
Project: EquityGuard

Purpose
This document explains how EquityGuard converts market signals into an educational risk score from 1 to 10.

Score Interpretation
1-3: Lower observed risk
The stock shows relatively stable movement, limited negative news, no obvious earnings event risk, and technical signals do not show strong stress.

4-6: Medium observed risk
The stock has mixed signals. There may be moderate volatility, uncertain news sentiment, unusual volume, technical weakness, or an upcoming event that needs attention.

7-10: Higher observed risk
The stock shows multiple risk signals, such as sharp price movement, unusual volume, negative news, earnings event risk, weak technical indicators, or concerning document-based risk factors.

Inputs Considered
EquityGuard may consider:
- recent price movement
- volume compared with average volume
- RSI
- MACD
- Bollinger Band position
- news sentiment
- earnings calendar
- retrieved company documents
- market or sector context
- uncertainty level

Technical Signal Guidance
RSI above 70 may suggest overbought conditions.
RSI below 30 may suggest oversold conditions.
A bearish MACD signal may indicate weakening momentum.
Price near or outside the Bollinger Bands may indicate elevated volatility.
Unusual volume may indicate market attention, news reaction, or institutional activity.

News Signal Guidance
Negative news may increase the risk score.
Neutral news may keep the score stable.
Positive news may reduce some risk but should not remove all risk.
No news after unusual price/volume movement should be treated as uncertainty, not safety.

Earnings Risk
Upcoming earnings can increase risk because price movement may become unpredictable before and after results.
If earnings are within the next 7 days, the assistant should highlight event risk.
If earnings have already passed, the assistant should check whether the market reaction is still visible in price movement or news.

Important Limitation
The score is not a prediction. It is an educational summary of observed risk signals at the time of analysis.
