Title: What Are Bollinger Bands?
Document Type: Market Education
Project: EquityGuard

Bollinger Bands are a technical indicator that show a price range around a moving average. They are often used to understand volatility and whether price is moving near the edge of its recent range.

Basic Structure
A Bollinger Band usually has:
- a middle moving average line
- an upper band
- a lower band

Common Interpretation
If price moves near the upper band, the stock may be extended compared with its recent range.
If price moves near the lower band, the stock may be under pressure compared with its recent range.
If the bands widen, volatility may be increasing.
If the bands narrow, volatility may be decreasing.

How EquityGuard Uses Bollinger Bands
EquityGuard uses Bollinger Bands to detect elevated volatility and unusual price positioning. This can affect the risk score when combined with news, earnings risk, volume, or other technical indicators.

Limitations
Touching a Bollinger Band does not automatically mean the stock will reverse. It is a signal that needs context.
