Title: What Is MACD?
Document Type: Market Education
Project: EquityGuard

MACD stands for Moving Average Convergence Divergence. It is a momentum indicator that compares short-term and longer-term price trends.

Basic Idea
MACD can help identify whether momentum is strengthening or weakening. Traders often look at the MACD line, signal line, and histogram.

Common Interpretation
- A bullish crossover may suggest improving momentum.
- A bearish crossover may suggest weakening momentum.
- A falling histogram may suggest momentum is fading.
- A rising histogram may suggest momentum is improving.

How EquityGuard Uses MACD
EquityGuard uses MACD as a supporting signal. A bearish MACD signal may increase the risk score if it appears together with negative news, falling price, high volume, or weak company context.

Limitations
MACD is based on historical price data. It can lag behind sudden news events and should not be treated as a prediction. EquityGuard should combine MACD with other evidence.
