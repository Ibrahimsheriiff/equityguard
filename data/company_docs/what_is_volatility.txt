Title: What Is Volatility?
Document Type: Market Education
Project: EquityGuard

Volatility means how much the price of an asset moves over time. A highly volatile stock can rise or fall quickly, while a less volatile stock usually moves in a smaller range.

Why Volatility Matters
Volatility is important because it affects uncertainty. A stock that moves sharply may create both opportunity and risk, but the movement itself does not automatically mean the company is good or bad.

Common Causes of Volatility
- earnings announcements
- interest rate changes
- company news
- sector-wide market movement
- macroeconomic data
- low liquidity
- investor sentiment
- unexpected events

How EquityGuard Uses Volatility
EquityGuard treats volatility as a risk signal, not a recommendation. High volatility can increase the risk score, especially when combined with negative news, unusual volume, weak momentum, or an upcoming earnings event.

Important Note
Volatility is not the same as risk, but it can be one part of risk. A stock may be volatile but still have strong fundamentals. Another stock may look stable but have hidden business or financial risks.
