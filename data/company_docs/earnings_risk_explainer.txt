Title: Earnings Risk Explainer
Document Type: Market Education
Project: EquityGuard

Earnings risk means the uncertainty connected to a company's upcoming or recently released financial results.

Why Earnings Matter
Company results can affect share prices because investors react to:
- revenue growth
- profit margins
- guidance
- management commentary
- cost pressure
- dividends
- outlook statements
- analyst expectations

Upcoming Earnings
If earnings are coming soon, the stock may become more volatile. Even strong companies can move sharply if results differ from market expectations.

Recent Earnings
If earnings were recently released, the agent should check whether the market is still reacting through price movement, volume, or news sentiment.

How EquityGuard Uses Earnings Risk
EquityGuard should increase the risk score when earnings are close and other signals are uncertain. It should explain that event risk can create short-term volatility.

Safe Explanation
EquityGuard should not predict the earnings result. It should only explain that the event may increase uncertainty.
